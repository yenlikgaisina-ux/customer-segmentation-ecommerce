# Customer Segmentation with Clustering
## Identifying Customer Segments for Churn Prevention in E-Commerce

---

### Business Question

**Which customer segments in a transnational e-commerce business are at risk of churn, and how can marketing efforts be tailored to retain, nurture, and re-engage them?**

Understanding and serving customers are two of the most important strategic marketing objectives in retail. This project applies unsupervised machine learning techniques to segment customers based on behavioural and demographic features derived from transactional data — enabling targeted marketing, improved customer retention, and smarter resource allocation.

---

### Dataset

- **Source:** UK E-commerce dataset — [Kaggle / UCI Machine Learning Repository](https://www.kaggle.com/)
- - **Coverage:** Transnational retail organisation, customers from 47 countries across 5 continents
  - - **Period:** January 2012 – December 2016
    - - **Size:** 951,668 rows, each representing a product ordered by a customer
     
      - ---

      ### Engineered Features

      Since raw transactional data cannot be directly used for customer-level clustering, five behavioural and demographic features were engineered at the customer level:

      | Feature | Description |
      |---|---|
      | **Recency** | Days since the customer's most recent purchase (current engagement) |
      | **Frequency** | Total number of unique orders placed (loyalty and repeat behaviour) |
      | **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)** | Total revenue generated by the customer across all orders |
      | **Average Unit Cost** | Mean price of products purchased (spending preferences) |
      | **Customer Age** | Derived from customer birthdate (demographic context) |

      ---

      ### Approach

      This project follows a structured, end-to-end data science workflow:

      #### 1. Data Preparation
      - Identified and assessed missing values in categorical fields (City, Postal Code, State/Province)
      - - Removed 21 fully duplicated transaction records
        - - Converted date fields to datetime format; cleaned and converted monetary fields (removing `$` and `,` symbols) to numeric
         
          - #### 2. Feature Engineering
          - - Aggregated transaction-level data to one row per customer
            - - Engineered Recency, Frequency, CLV, Average Unit Cost, and Customer Age
              - - Applied z-score standardisation (StandardScaler) prior to clustering
               
                - #### 3. Optimal Cluster Selection (k)
                - Three complementary methods were used to determine k=5:
                - - **Elbow Method** — Diminishing WCSS improvement beyond k=5
                  - - **Silhouette Score** — Peaked at k=5 (score: ~0.263), indicating strong cluster separation
                    - - **Hierarchical Clustering** — Ward linkage dendrogram on a 1,000-customer sample supported ~5 natural groupings
                     
                      - #### 4. K-Means Clustering
                      - - Final model applied to 68,300 customers with k=5
                        - - Cluster assignments visualised via boxplots across all five features
                         
                          - #### 5. Dimensionality Reduction & Visual Validation
                          - - **PCA** — First two components explain ~65% of variance (PC1=44.34%, PC2=20.75%); partial cluster separation visible
                            - - **t-SNE** — Applied to 20,000-customer sample; shows clearer, more compact cluster separation than PCA
                             
                              - ---

                              ### Cluster Profiles

                              | Cluster | Profile | Key Characteristics |
                              |---|---|---|
                              | **0** | High-Value Loyalists | Low recency, high frequency, highest CLV — ideal for retention and loyalty programmes |
                              | **1** | Growth Potential | Younger customers, moderate frequency and CLV — target with personalised promotions |
                              | **2** | At-Risk / Churners | High recency, low frequency, lowest CLV — priority for re-engagement campaigns |
                              | **3** | Premium Buyers | Low frequency but highest average unit cost — responsive to high-value product recommendations |
                              | **4** | Steady Older Customers | Moderate frequency and CLV, older age — sustain with consistent service and tailored comms |

                              ---

                              ### Key Findings

                              - K-Means with k=5 produced clearly interpretable and business-actionable segments
                              - - Frequency and CLV showed the strongest differentiation across clusters
                                - - Cluster 2 (~at-risk customers) represents the highest churn risk and the most urgent marketing opportunity
                                  - - t-SNE provided the clearest visual confirmation of cluster separation, outperforming PCA for local structure
                                    - - The combination of statistical (IQR/elbow), ML (silhouette/K-Means), and visual (PCA/t-SNE) validation builds high confidence in the segmentation

                                      ### What a Business Would Do With This

                                      A marketing or CRM team could:
                                      - **Prioritise Cluster 0** for loyalty scheme investments and exclusive retention offers
                                      - - **Nurture Cluster 1** with targeted digital campaigns and personalised product recommendations
                                        - - **Launch win-back campaigns** for Cluster 2 with time-limited offers or satisfaction surveys
                                          - - **Surface premium products** to Cluster 3 customers via email and recommendation engines
                                            - - **Maintain Cluster 4** through reliable service, straightforward communications, and seasonal promotions
